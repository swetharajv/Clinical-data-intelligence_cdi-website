What we track

The numbers dental and GP clinics miss most.

These are sitting inside your practice management software right now. CDI reads them, connects them, and tells you what they mean.

Associate revenue per hour

Which associates generate the most revenue per clinical hour — not per session. The gap between your highest and lowest performer is often £60–100/hr.

Chair utilisation by day and time

Which chairs and which time slots are consistently underperforming in revenue. A chair that looks booked often isn’t generating proportional revenue.

Private vs NHS revenue split

The actual split between private and NHS work by associate, by day, and over time — so you can see whether your private conversion is growing, stalling, or declining.

Treatment uptake trends

Which treatments were popular six months ago and have since gone quiet — and what the monthly revenue gap looks like if you restored them to baseline.

Patient recall failure rate

What percentage of patients due for recall have not rebooked, broken down by associate. Each missed recall appointment is a specific revenue figure, not an abstract problem.

Monthly written intelligence memo

A written report every month. Not charts — sentences. One specific finding, one specific number, one specific action. This is the part no software provides.

No patient data required
